Problem

Most teams can describe Shadow IT and Shadow AI risk, but cannot govern it as an operating system.

The hard part is not recognizing that unapproved SaaS and AI use is happening. The hard part is proving what apps are in play, which are approved, what data is flowing into them, what the vendor posture looks like, and who owns the next decision.

SaaS and AI use is visible in fragments, but inventory, ownership, spend, and review state are rarely kept in one working system.
Consultant teams spend time rebuilding the same governance structure for every client instead of delivering from one repeatable workflow.
Leadership sees presentations, but not a live decision register with confidence, blind spots, and re-review dates.
Vendor posture questions are left half-answered, so sanctioned vs unsanctioned use remains operationally muddy.

Workflow

Evidence → inventory → posture → decision → action → report.

The workflow starts with imports and honest coverage scoring. It turns available evidence into client-facing governance output without pretending every blind spot is solved.

Step 01

Normalize intake evidence

Import structured evidence from workspace OAuth, IdP/SSO, finance spend, browser/endpoint, CASB/proxy, and vendor/GRC exports.

Step 02

Build the app inventory

Map SaaS, AI, browser extensions, OAuth apps, and unknown tools by business unit, approval state, spend, data sensitivity, source, and review state.

Step 03

Record vendor posture and allowed use

Capture first-pass posture on contract state, DPA, deletion rights, residency, AI training policy, security docs, and what is approved, restricted, consolidated, or blocked.

Step 04

Assign decisions and owner actions

Route work to Security, Privacy, Procurement, Finance, Legal, TPRM, or Audit with conditions, rationale, review date, analyst notes, and clear accountability.

Step 05

Publish leadership-ready output

Generate a concise operating pack: risk register, owner action plan, evidence coverage, decision register, and executive summary.
